# Mothership (v1.2) — One-Page Core Rules Reference

## Dice
- **d100**: All Stat Checks and Saves
- **d20**: Panic Die (Panic Checks only)
- **90–99** on d100 is always a failure
- **Doubles** (11, 22, …, 99) are Criticals

---

## Stats
- Strength
- Speed
- Intellect
- Combat

**Stat Check**
- Roll **1d100 ≤ Stat**
- Failure → +1 Stress
- Critical Success (double, success): something very good
- Critical Failure (double, fail): something bad + Panic Check

---

## Saves
- Sanity
- Fear
- Body

**Save**
- Roll **1d100 ≤ Save**
- Used as reactions to danger/trauma
- Failure → +1 Stress
- 90–99 always fail

---

## Skills
- Trained: **+10**
- Expert: **+15**
- Master: **+20**

Applied by adding bonus to Stat before rolling.

---

## Advantage / Disadvantage
- **Advantage [+]**: Roll twice, take best
- **Disadvantage [−]**: Roll twice, take worst
- Applies to Stat Checks, Saves, Panic Checks

---

## Stress
- Gained on failed Stat Checks and Saves
- Current Stress starts at **2**
- Higher Stress increases chance of Panic
- Stress is required to improve Saves

---

## Panic
**Panic Check**
- Roll **1d20**
- Must roll **> current Stress** to avoid Panic
- Failure → roll on Panic Table

Triggered by:
- Critical Failures
- Extreme stress events
- Warden discretion

---

## Class Trauma Responses
- **Marine**: When you Panic, all close allies make a Fear Save
- **Android**: Close allies’ Fear Saves are at Disadvantage
- **Scientist**: When you fail a Sanity Save, close allies gain +1 Stress
- **Teamster**: Once per session, gain Advantage on a Panic Check

---

## Health & Wounds
- Damage reduces Health
- At 0 Health → gain 1 Wound, Health resets
- Wounds ≥ Max Wounds → Death Save

---

## Core Loop Summary
1. Describe intent
2. Roll only if failure matters
3. Failures add Stress
4. Stress leads to Panic
5. Panic creates cascading consequences

---

## Damage
- Damage reduces **Health**
- If Health > 0: no Wound
- If Health ≤ 0:
  - Gain **1 Wound**
  - Health resets to max (minus excess damage)

---

## Wounds
- Each Wound applies a serious consequence
- Roll on **Wounds Table**
- Wounds are persistent
- Wounds ≥ Max Wounds → Death Save

---

## Death
- When Wounds reach Max Wounds:
  - Roll **Body Save**
  - Success: alive but incapacitated
  - Failure: death

---

## Armor & Cover
- Ignore damage ≤ (Armor + Cover)
- Damage exceeding Armor:
  - Destroys armor or cover
  - Excess damage carries over
- Damage Reduction reduces incoming damage before comparison

---

## Critical Results
- **Critical Success** (double, success):
  - Extra effectiveness
  - Faster, cleaner, or safer outcome
- **Critical Failure** (double, failure):
  - Complication occurs
  - Immediate Panic Check required

---

## Panic Outcomes
- Panic effects may include:
  - Freezing, fleeing, screaming
  - Attacking allies
  - Long-term mental Conditions
  - Rare moments of extreme clarity

---

## Conditions
- Long-term effects from Panic or Wounds
- Require treatment, rest, or narrative resolution
- Persist across sessions if untreated

---

## Stress Floor
- Characters have a **Minimum Stress**
- Stress cannot be reduced below this value
- Minimum Stress increases over time

---

## Healing
- Medical care can restore Health
- Wounds require treatment and time

---

# Mothership (v1.2) — Tertiary Rules Reference

## Time & Pressure
- Time pressure is narrative-first
- Delays may trigger Saves or additional Stress
- Environmental hazards (oxygen, radiation) create turn-based pressure

---

## Encumbrance
- Characters have limited inventory slots
- Exceeding limits prevents picking up new items
- Containers may increase capacity

---

## Oxygen & Vacuum
- Lack of oxygen triggers **Body Saves**
- Failure causes rapid Health loss
- Vacuum exposure is immediately lethal without protection

---

## Radiation
- Exposure requires **Body Saves**
- Failure may cause:
  - Stress
  - Wounds
  - Long-term Conditions
- Effects may be delayed

---

## Weapons
- Melee weapons are silent
- Firearms are loud and attract attention
- Heavy weapons risk collateral damage

---

## NPCs & Monsters
- Have four stats:
  - **Combat**: Attack rolls
  - **Instinct**: Behavior trigger/reaction
  - **HP**: Health Points
  - **Wounds**: Max Wounds before death
- Most cannot be reasoned with

**Conversational NPCs:**
- Have **Attitude** (-5 to +5, starts at 0)
- Dialogue trees with 2-4 options per node:
  - **Barks**: Flavor responses, no roll, may shift Attitude ±1
  - **Stat Checks**: Require Social/Intellect/Combat check
    - Success → info/item unlocked, Attitude +1
    - Failure → no reward, Attitude -1, +1 Stress
  - **Conditional**: Only visible if Attitude/stats meet threshold
- Critical Success → +2 Attitude + bonus outcome
- Critical Failure → hostile reaction or flee
- Conversation costs your action for the turn

**Attitude Effects:**
- +3 or higher: NPC joins party or provides major aid
- -3 or lower: NPC attacks or flees
- 0: Neutral, willing to talk but cautious

---

## Rest
- Rest action:
  - Restores Health
  - Reduces Stress
  - Does not remove Wounds
- Unsafe rest may trigger encounters

---

## Advancement
- Characters improve Saves, not Stats
- **Spend 5 Stress → +1 Save**
- Survival is the primary reward

---
